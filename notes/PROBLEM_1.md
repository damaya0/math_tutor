# Problem 1 Solution

To solve the expression \( 2 \times 7 - \frac{9}{3} + 8 \), we can break it down step by step:

1. **Multiply:** 
   - Calculate \( 2 \times 7 = 14 \)

2. **Divide:** 
   - Calculate \( 9 \div 3 = 3 \)

3. **Subtract:** 
   - Now subtract the division result from the multiplication result:
   - Calculate \( 14 - 3 = 11 \)

4. **Add:** 
   - Finally, add 8 to the result:
   - Calculate \( 11 + 8 = 19 \)

Thus, the final answer is \( 19 \).
